2018 in Review, in Texts

Some highlights of my year, told only through actual texts I sent and received.

WTF why?

I guess the easiest way to explain is just to share a litany of things I've been thinking about lately: how technology mediates my and other people's lives; the inherent knowledge and wisdom of being trans, being a person of color; how that wisdom is often only shared and accessed in my private conversations; how so much of the writing people do now is through texts, on Twitter, in emails; how that's often not viewed as "real" writing, but why shouldn't it be?; how people often cultivate a persona or presence for public social media and how that can differ in what I might call private social media, like text messages or emails.

I'm also thinking about the limitations of looking back and reflecting on a year in terms of achievements or "big moments", in what ways that view is limited; about how those limitations shift when I instead focus on the supposedly mundane day-to-day communication that happens privately between me and another person ‚Äì how that view is limited too, but in different ways; how it's just a different slice of the picture, a different viewfinder that eclipses certain things while revealing others.

I guess inherent in this is also some thinking about how the internet sees me, when its perspective is made up only of the things I search on Google, "like" on Facebook, or follow on Instagram. What does that view of a person look like? I'm remembering back to when I downloaded Facebook's big zip of data on me, and flipped through the advertising "interests" it had recorded for me. One was "Transmission, Automobiles". This was a pretty comically off-base interpretation of me liking the page for "Transmission", a retreat for trans-masculine people. What other ways are my interests misinterpreted or mistaken? Missed altogether? And how would that picture change if (when?) tech companies have access to all of these private messages? When I think of how the internet views me, I picture a strange hologram version of me ‚Äì¬†certain interests and traits emphasized to unrealistic proportion, others just holes in the picture.

But still, I think I feel more visible in the day-to-day life and relationships conveyed in these conversations than any "big achievements" list I could write.
